For this homework, I displayed the world map in nine different projections. I used WGS84, the Aitoff Projection, EPSG: 3857, 53018, 54034, 54027, and 102016. Then, I chose the Bonne Projection and the Eckert II Projection for my additional two projections. Some projections are equal area projections, meaning they keep relative size accurate throughout the map. Some projections are equidistant, meaning they keep distance scale along lines on the map.

The Earth is a a three dimensional object, so all two dimensional maps have distortion in at least one of the following categories: shape, area, distance, and direction. No projection is perfect, so for this homework assignment, I looked at different world projections to see how they exhibited distortion of the Earth's surface.

In order to display the map in different projections using QGIS, I first downloaded the data and map files. Then, using QGIS I changed the projections.

WGS84 Projection

My observations: This projection has distortion of area and shape at the poles, while the areas nearest the equator have the least distortion.

Aitoff Projection

My observations: This projection is a "compromise" projection, meaning it is not equal area or equal distance. This projection shows distortion of shape, area, distance and direction.

Pseudo-Mercator Projection

My observations: This map shows the true shape of countries, but has distortion of area, esspecially near the poles. An example can be seen in Greenland, which has an exaggerated area. However, there is not distortion of direction.

Winkel Projection

My observations: This projection is also a "compromise" and has distortion of shape, area and direction. This projection is better for world maps than small scale/local maps.

Equal Area Cylindrical Projection

My observations: This is an equal area projection. It does have distortion of shape, area and direction. The distortion increases as the distance from the equator increases.

Equidistant Conic Projection

My observations: Since this is a conic projection, it uses standard parallels. So, distortion of shape and area increases as distance from the standard parallels increases.

North Pole Azimuthal Equidistant Projection

My observations: In this projection, the distortion of shape and area increases as distance from the center point increases.

Bonne Projection

My observations: I chose this projection because it is almost heart-shaped. The Bonne projection equal-area but has distortion of direction, distance, angles and shapes.

Eckert II Projection

My observations: I chose this projection because of its sharp angles. The shape of this map is very unusual, as most maps have rounded, more natural seeming edges. This map is an equal-area projection but has distortion of direction, distance, angles and shapes.
